Conclusion: Applying Test Techniques

In this course, we explored several test techniques, including black-box, white-box, and experience-based testing techniques. Each of these methods plays a vital role in ensuring software quality by identifying different types of defects.

Key Takeaways

  • Black-box Testing: Focuses on system behavior and does not require knowledge of the internal structure of the system.
  • White-box Testing: Requires an understanding of the internal structure or code and helps test specific paths and conditions.
  • Experience-based Testing: Relies on the experience and intuition of the tester to explore possible defects, especially when documentation is limited.
  • Test Technique Selection: The appropriate test technique depends on the specific system under test, the level of risk, and the available resources.
